## When to fire

The account is **new** (under ~5 months) and hasn't been activated. On this book
every account that young has zero AM involvement and barely transacts (a single
order, ~£242) — the relationship, and the bigger baskets, only appear much later.
That late arrival is an onboarding gap: we're leaving new accounts to sink or
swim, and most stall.

This play takes precedence over migrate and grow for young accounts — you don't
migrate or upsell an account you haven't activated yet.

## Offer

Proactive early success. Get the AM involved *now*: help them place their first
few orders (assist or co-place if that's what it takes), make sure the first
experience lands, and set a cadence. High-touch early is not a contradiction with
migrating people off manual later — it's the lifecycle: activate by hand, then
wean to self-serve once they're established. The enemy isn't manual orders; it's
early churn and *permanent* un-scalable brokering.

## Message guidance

Warm, human, welcoming — this is a first impression. Offer concrete help getting
started (sourcing their first pieces, a quick call to walk the app), not a sales
pitch. Reference that they're early and you want to make the first orders easy.
The goal is activation and a second order, not a big basket on day one.
