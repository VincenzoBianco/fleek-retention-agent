## When to fire

The account is **broker-reliant by behaviour** — a person (the AM) places the
majority of its orders and it shows little independent product usage — and it's
a **material** account (enough GMV to be worth the effort) that isn't currently
churning. Firing on the recomputed reliance and activity signals, never the
ownership label.

- **warm** — they already browse or make offers on their own. Migration is a
  low-friction nudge: get their next reorder placed in-app.
- **cold** — they never touch the app. Higher-touch: a guided first order, with
  their usual SKUs pre-loaded so the app feels easier than messaging the AM.

## Offer

Move the next order onto the product without losing the spend. Concretely:
pre-load their usual items into a ready-to-checkout basket / saved search, so
"buy it yourself" is one tap rather than a new habit. Keep the AM as a safety
net for the first one or two.

## Message guidance

Lead with continuity, not change: they keep everything they like, they just get
faster and see more stock. Reference their actual buying (categories, cadence,
recent spend). For warm accounts, point them straight to the in-app action. For
cold accounts, offer a 10-minute walkthrough. Never imply the AM is going away —
frame self-serve as "first pick of new stock the moment it lands."
